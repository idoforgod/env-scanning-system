**Significance Score: 5/5** | **Confidence: 95%**

### Signal
**Pemvidutide (Altimmune) Receives FDA Breakthrough Designation for MASH (January 5, 2026)**

### STEEPS Category
Primary: **Technological** | Secondary: **Economic**

### What Happened
Altimmune announced FDA Breakthrough Therapy Designation for pemvidutide, a balanced 1:1 glucagon/GLP-1 dual receptor agonist for metabolic dysfunction-associated steatohepatitis (MASH). Designation based on Phase 2b IMPACT trial showing:
- Statistically significant MASH resolution without worsening fibrosis
- Sustained weight loss at 48 weeks without plateau
- Fewer adverse-event discontinuations than placebo

### Why It's a Weak Signal
- **Mechanism Innovation**: First drug activating both glucagon (direct hepatic effects) AND GLP-1 (metabolic effects) in 1:1 ratio - novel therapeutic class beyond current GLP-1 agents
- **Disease Expansion Path**: MASH only beginning; pathway opens to alcohol-associated liver disease and non-alcoholic fatty liver disease (NAFLD)
- **Regulatory Confidence**: FDA Breakthrough designation indicates conviction that dual approach provides "substantial improvement over available therapies"
- **Market Scale**: MASH affects ~25M Americans; liver disease is silent epidemic with no approved treatments

### Significance
**Paradigm Shift Signal**: Marks transition from single-pathway targeting to multi-mechanism optimization. Expects replication across other disease areas. Points toward future of "rational polypharmacy" - designing drugs that hit multiple pathways simultaneously rather than sequentially.
